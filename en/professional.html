Internship · Feb. 2025 – Aug. 2025 · Rungis, FR
Systems Integration Engineer
Thales LAS

Context

TopSky-ATC, an air traffic management solution developed by Thales, currently ensures the safety of nearly 40% of the world’s airspace.

Within the Integration team — responsible for ensuring integration, deployment, and supervision of releases — I worked on an internal tool dedicated to building, distributing, and installing product versions.

Problem

The existing tool showed its limits: security-wise exchanges to improve, incomplete traceability, lack of centralization, and manual, time-consuming steps for integrators.

Goal

Design and deploy a secure, automated pipeline based on HTTPS and Artifactory to publish and retrieve versions, while centralizing artifacts and strengthening traceability.

Missions

Scoping: requirements gathering, feasibility study, target architecture choice.

Design & deployment: implementation of an HTTPS + Artifactory solution.

Tool evolutions:
publish – automatic publication of versions to Artifactory (via HTTPS).
import – retrieval and installation from Artifactory onto target machines.

Testing: unit tests and non-regression tests.

CI/CD integration (Jenkins): automated unit tests and also the creation of a vApp template in the catalog.

Documentation: drafting of technical specifications and user documentation.

Tools & Methods

Python, Bash, Jenkins, Git (Github/Bitbucket), MobaXterm, Artifactory, Linux (RHEL7), Apache, SSL/TLS certificates, VMware.

Agile methodology (Jira), coordination with Configuration/Integration teams.

Results

Implementation of a centralized, secure, and automated chain for managing product versions.

Reduction of manual operations and improved release traceability.

A robust and tailored solution for the team, ready for use in operational environments.

Internship · Apr. 2024 – Aug. 2024 · Vélizy, FR
Industry 4.0 Engineer
Thales AVS

Context

Thales Avionics, one of Thales’ largest industrial sites in France with over 2,500 employees. I joined the Industry 4.0 team directly attached to the plant.

My mission focused on evolving digital production tools with a dual focus: supervision of industrial equipment (benches, ovens, pumps, baths, etc.) and collection of the plant’s machines’ energy data.

Problem

Two main challenges guided this internship:

  • An industrial supervision software experiencing malfunctions in display (unrecognized machines, inconsistent data, wrong states), complicating real-time monitoring for operators.

  • The need to integrate machines into a new energy collection software to ensure reliable consumption reporting. This project aligned with the plant’s 2030 carbon footprint reduction roadmap, centralizing data to pilot energy consumption.

Goal

Improve the reliability of the industrial supervision software, and deploy a new energy collection tool, to guarantee reliable data reporting that is centralized and usable by operators and the plant.

Missions

Industrial supervision:

  • Analyze and fix malfunctions: machine connection failures, missing or inconsistent data, display errors.
  • Optimize communication between equipment and PCs via a Siemens module (TCP/IP).

Data collection:

  • Integrate machines into the new consumption collection tool.
  • Verify the reliability and usability of energy data.
  • Contribute to documentation and follow-ups on changes.

Tools & Methods

Technical: industrial supervision, TCP/IP protocols, Siemens module, energy collection software, Python (JSON file handling, multi-threading, MQTT), Bash.

Methods: direct interaction with operators, incremental analyze-and-fix approach.

Results

Supervision software made more robust for operators (fewer errors and better consistency of machine data).

Effective deployment of the energy collection software, enabling automated and usable data reporting.

Contribution to the Industry 4.0 strategy and to the 2030 carbon footprint reduction objectives of the Vélizy site.
