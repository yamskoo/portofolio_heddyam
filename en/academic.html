National Robotics Competition · RobAFIS

French Association for Systems Engineering

AFIS, an affiliate member of the International Council on Systems Engineering (INCOSE), has brought together major industrial players since 1998 around best practices in systems engineering.

The RobAFIS competition challenges teams to design a complete robotic system that meets a given requirements brief. Each team delivers a solution spanning hardware, software, and human factors, applying systems engineering methods across the entire project lifecycle.

The final evaluates both the quality of the design dossier and the operational performance of the robot.

These are the two editions I took part in:

  • 2024: Robot performing binary calculations and displaying the result with tokens.
  • 2025: Robot for waste sorting and transport within a time limit.

Qt C++ HMI V&V Systems BLE

Our team achieved 4th place among about ten French engineering schools (including ENSA, CY Tech and ISAE-Supméca). It was an immersive systems engineering experience, combining rigor, creativity and teamwork.

I appreciated the balance between the technical side—robot design and programming— and the methodological side—a complete, professional design dossier.

In the first edition, I contributed to the robot design and the dossier. In the second edition, as team lead, I coordinated the project, with strong involvement in the design dossier and the HMI.

This journey strengthened my technical and organizational skills, while developing real leadership and team spirit.
